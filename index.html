Build Philosophy
This build trades the Bull's Roar resets for a more active Fury economy. By investing in Channeled Blades' Fury-cost passive you get a 6-second cooldown instead of relying on a reset — meaning consistent AoE damage with less downtime. The rotation revolves around generating enough Fury to fuel repeated Channeled Blades casts while layering CC, debuffs, and sustained pressure in between. You still provide full team utility — Haste, Precision, Empower, and AoE CC break for allies.

Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
  • Self-sustaining Fury loop with no reliance on external resets
  • Consistent AoE damage every 6s via Channeled Blades Fury passive
  • Strong CC chain with Stun uptime from Cataclysm + Rupture
  • Fissure Mark amplifies all damage by 10% on top of the burst window
  • Still provides full team utility — Haste, Precision, Empower, AoE CC break
Weaknesses
  • More ability management required — Fury economy needs attention
  • Weaker if Prelude or Cataclysm is on cooldown mid-fight
  • Relies on Fury-cost passive node on Channeled Blades
  • Less effective when enemies break out of CC before burst lands
When to Use This Build
Use When
  • You want consistent AoE damage with a tight self-sufficient rotation
  • You are comfortable managing Fury actively through a rotation
  • Fights are prolonged and sustained DPS matters more than one-shot burst
  • Team needs a frontline that enables follow-up and provides utility
Avoid When
  • You are unfamiliar with the Fury economy — mistakes are punishing
  • Fights end too quickly for the full rotation to pay off
  • Targets need to be isolated and burst individually

Build Philosophy
This build replaces Onslaught with Crimson Raze and trades the Channeled Blades Fury-cost passive for the +80 Fury passive instead. The concept revolves around banking enough Fury for two consecutive Raze casts (240 Fury total) to land on a grouped cluster — shorter burst window but significantly higher peak damage when it lands. Channeled Blades becomes your primary Fury generator rather than a damage tool, and Bull's Roar resets it to extend generation time. You still provide full team utility — Haste from Bull's Roar, Precision and Empower from Rising Fury, and AoE CC break for allies.
Core Rotation
Round 1 — Engage & Generate
Rising Fury
Activate first. Grants Precision (+20% crit) and Empower to nearby allies. Generates 80 Fury.
→
Prelude → Savage Charge → Heroic Leap
Launch into the cluster. Hinders enemies −35% move speed. Chain mobility skills to close any remaining gap. Cataclysm follows — stuns and refunds Fury cost via passive.
Prelude 1,710 · Cataclysm 2,040 · Hinder 4s · Stun 2s
→
Fissure → Rupture
Fissure applies Mark (+10% damage taken). Rupture stuns enemies for 2s and grants you Celerity (+25% move speed).
Fissure 1,965 · Rupture 1,995 · Mark 6s · Stun 2s
→
Channeled Blades
Primary Fury generator. 6 ticks generating 10 Fury each = 60 Fury per cast. This is your resource builder, not your damage tool.
DMG 1,020×6 · +10 Fury/tick · Radius 4.8m
→
Bull's Roar → Channeled Blades
Bull's Roar resets Channeled Blades immediately and grants Haste to allies. Second cast generates another 60 Fury. Combined with passives you should be approaching 240 Fury.
Bull's Roar 1,965 · Haste −25% activation · 3s
Round 2 — Double Raze
Crimson Raze × 2
When you hit 240 Fury — fire both Raze casts in quick succession on the grouped cluster. This is the peak damage window of this build. Two casts can swing a team fight.
DMG 2,300 per cast · Empower (3) · Cost 120 Fury each
→
Gash + Hemorrhage + Autos
Fill downtime while rebuilding Fury for the next window. Gash reapplies Hinder, Hemorrhage maintains Bleed. Keep ticking toward the next Double Raze.

Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
  • Highest peak burst damage on grouped targets of any Champion build
  • Channeled Blades as a Fury generator creates a reliable resource loop
  • Still provides full team utility — Haste, Precision, Empower, AoE CC break
  • Double Raze on a big pull can single-handedly swing a team fight
Weaknesses
  • Lower sustained DPS — rotation is built around banking, not constant output
  • Requires patience — Double Raze only fires when 240 Fury is banked
  • Punishing if enemies spread out before the Raze window lands
  • Most demanding Fury management of any build
When to Use This Build
Use When
  • Fights involve large grouped clusters where Double Raze hits multiple targets
  • Your team can hold enemies in position long enough to bank Fury
  • You want maximum burst impact in a coordinated engage
Avoid When
  • Enemies are mobile and hard to group
  • Fights are short — you won't have time to bank two full Raze casts
  • You need consistent sustained damage rather than a big burst window
